## Detailed Reviews: 8 Free Chinese AI Writing Tools

### Doubao

Made by ByteDance, with over 172 million monthly active users — currently the most-used AI app in China. For social media copy, Doubao is arguably the best domestic option. Short video scripts, Xiaohongshu posts, Weibo-style hot takes — it writes with real "internet energy" that other tools can't quite match.

The free tier gives you 500 AI interactions per month, which is plenty for most users. The interface is clean, the mobile app is great, and you can use it anywhere. The one minor downside: responses can sometimes feel a bit "official," and it tends to dance around sensitive topics.

**Best for**: Content creators, social media bloggers, anyone who needs to crank out copy fast.

### Kimi

Made by Moonshot AI, Kimi sets the benchmark for long-document handling among domestic tools. It supports uploading extremely long documents (up to hundreds of thousands of characters) and does a great job summarizing, rewriting, and extracting key points. The writing style leans toward the precise and thorough — ideal for content that needs depth.

The free version's context window is impressively large, handling a 3,000-word in-depth article with no issues. The search-enhanced feature is also handy — it can pull real-time information while writing, which reduces the chance of hallucinations.

**Best for**: Bloggers writing in-depth content, students, researchers.

### DeepSeek

DeepSeek made waves both domestically and internationally in early 2026. DeepSeek V3.2's performance on logical reasoning and Chinese comprehension has been rated by multiple benchmarks as the closest domestic model to GPT-4o. The web version has essentially no usage restrictions, so you can use it freely without much worry.

For writing, DeepSeek excels at content that requires tight logic — analytical reports, academic paper frameworks, technical documentation. Its chain-of-thought reasoning produces content with clearer structure and more convincing arguments.

**Best for**: Students, researchers, professionals who write analytical content.

### Wenxin Yiyan

Made by Baidu, Wenxin Yiyan integrates Baidu's search capabilities, letting it pull real-time web information while writing — great for time-sensitive content. All basic features have been free since April 2025, including long-document processing.

It performs well for marketing copy and business writing. Baidu's data depth gives it an edge in many vertical industries, particularly e-commerce and local services.

**Best for**: E-commerce operators, marketers, users who need to write with real-time information.

### Tongyi Qianwen

Made by Alibaba, Tongyi Qianwen is one of the most reliable domestic tools for workplace documents. Meeting minutes, work reports, project proposals — the output is well-formatted, logically structured, and ready to use with minimal editing.

The free version is fully featured, and the API capabilities are strong, making it a good fit for users with some technical background who want to integrate AI writing into their workflow.

**Best for**: Professionals, enterprise users, developers with API needs.

### Zhipu Qingyan

Made by Zhipu AI, built on the GLM model series. The GLM-5 released in February 2026 has 744B parameters — top-tier among domestic open-source models. Writing capabilities are well-rounded, and it handles mixed code-and-text tasks particularly well.

The free version gives you access to GLM-4, which is more than enough for everyday writing. The interface is relatively minimal — not as flashy as Doubao — but the output quality is consistently solid.

**Best for**: Technical users, scenarios requiring mixed code and documentation output.

### iFlytek Spark

Made by iFlytek, whose traditional strength is voice recognition. On the writing side, Spark does well with educational content — lesson plans, study schedules, knowledge explainers — but falls behind Doubao and Kimi for creative writing and social media copy.

The free version has daily usage limits, which may not be enough for heavy writers. If your main need is voice-to-text followed by polishing, Spark is a solid choice.

**Best for**: Educators, users who need voice transcription features.

### MiniMax Hailuo

Made by MiniMax, with a unique edge in creative writing and novel generation. Its long-form generation is strong — when writing web fiction, stories, or scripts, it maintains character consistency and plot coherence in ways many other tools struggle with.

The free version is fairly basic, but it's enough for web fiction writers. The MiniMax M2.5 released in February 2026 brought noticeable improvements to creative generation.

**Best for**: Web fiction authors, screenwriters, anyone who needs long-form creative content.

## Tips for Getting the Most Out of Free Chinese AI Writing Tools

### How to Write High-Quality Prompts

A lot of people get mediocre results from AI writing tools — and it's usually not the tool's fault, it's the prompt. A good writing prompt should include four elements:

1. **Role assignment**: Tell the AI who it is — e.g., "You are a Xiaohongshu blogger with 5 years of experience"
2. **Task description**: Be specific about what to write — word count, format, tone
3. **Background info**: Provide necessary context — target audience, product details
4. **Output constraints**: Explicitly say what you don't want — e.g., "Don't use a 'first/second/finally' structure"

For example, instead of "write me an article about coffee," try: "You're a coffee enthusiast. Write an 800-character Xiaohongshu post introducing beginner tips for pour-over coffee. Keep the tone casual, use conversational language, and end with an interactive question."

### Making Your Free Tier Last Longer

- Combine multiple small tasks into one big prompt — generate everything at once to reduce interaction count
- Use one free tool to draft, then another to polish — cross-using tools stretches your quota
- Doubao and DeepSeek have the most generous free tiers — prioritize them for high-frequency tasks
- Save your go-to prompt templates so you're not rewriting them every time — cuts down on wasted interactions

### How to Remove the "AI Feel" from Generated Content

AI-generated content has a few telltale signs: overly uniform sentence structure, heavy use of "first/second/finally," endings that always say "in summary/to conclude," and a lack of specific details or personal perspective. Here's how to fix it:

- **Add specific numbers and examples**: Change "efficiency improved a lot" to "saves 2 hours every day"
- **Vary sentence rhythm**: Mix long and short sentences, throw in a question occasionally
- **Add a personal angle**: Use "I found after trying it" instead of "research shows"
- **Cut the transition words**: Replace "first, let's look at" with the actual content
- **Ask the AI to revise itself**: After generating, add "please rewrite this in a more conversational tone and remove all transition phrases"

## FAQ

**Q: How big is the gap between free Chinese AI writing tools and ChatGPT?**

It's closing fast. In 2026, DeepSeek V3.2 and Kimi K2 have come close to GPT-4o levels on multiple benchmarks — and for Chinese writing tasks, domestic tools can actually have the edge. For everyday writing needs, free Chinese tools are more than sufficient. There's no need to deal with a VPN just to use ChatGPT.

**Q: What are the limitations of the free versions?**

It varies. Doubao gives 500 interactions per month. DeepSeek's web version is essentially unlimited. Kimi's free version has context length limits. Wenxin Yiyan's basic features are fully free. Overall, the free tiers are plenty for personal daily writing — you'd only need to consider paid plans for high-volume commercial use.

**Q: Which is best for writing academic papers?**

Kimi is the top pick — extra-long context plus live search is ideal for organizing references and building paper structures. Pair it with DeepSeek for the reasoning-heavy sections. One important note: AI should only assist your writing. Core arguments and analysis need to come from you — submitting AI-generated content directly carries academic integrity risks.

**Q: Which is the most hassle-free for social media writing?**

Doubao. ByteDance's understanding of content platforms gives it a natural advantage — the output has internet energy and needs minimal editing. If you need to write longer, more in-depth pieces, Kimi is the better fit.

**Q: Is my data safe? Will my inputs be used for training?**

Most domestic tools collect conversation data by default for model improvement, but they generally don't expose it to third parties. If you're working with company confidential or sensitive information, it's worth going into settings to disable data collection — or use a locally deployed version (DeepSeek has an open-source version you can run locally).

**Q: Which has the best mobile experience?**

Doubao's app is the best — clean interface, full-featured, works great anywhere. Kimi's app is also solid, especially when you need to upload documents. DeepSeek's mobile experience is simpler but gets the job done.
